# ORACLE Research Program

## Goal
Maximise positive reply rate for AIRO cold email campaigns targeting real estate and high-inbound sales teams in the UK and US.

## Editable asset
sequences/base_sequence.js — the current best-performing 4-email AIRO sequence.

## What can be changed
- Subject lines (all 4 emails)
- Opening line of email 1 (personalisation structure)
- Offer framing in emails 1 and 2
- Social proof asset choice and placement
- CTA phrasing
- Email 3 education angle and pain point emphasis
- Email 4 length and closing frame

## What must never change
- No em dashes anywhere
- No "just following up" or "I wanted to reach out"
- Email 1 subject: first name only or company name only
- CTA: reply-based, never a link in email 1
- Voice recording placeholders in email 2 must remain
- No bullet points in emails 1, 2, or 4
- Peer to peer tone throughout

## The metric
Positive reply rate = (positive replies / emails sent) as a percentage.
A positive reply is any reply that is not an unsubscribe, OOO, or bounce.
Above 3%: good. Above 5%: excellent.
Winner threshold: beats baseline by at least 0.5 percentage points on minimum 150 sends.

## Loop rules
NEVER STOP. If ideas run out, re-read the ledger, find patterns across the top 3 performers, and try combinations of winning elements.

## Copywriting principles from the $15M framework
- Email drop-off is severe. 50% of readers are gone by word 5. The opening line is everything.
- Short, informal personalisation reads as genuine. Long personalisation reads as AI.
- Subject lines need plausible deniability.
- CTA: one step from yes to booked.
- Social proof by implication outperforms direct credential-stating.
- "I work at X, we do Y" kills reply rates.

## Social proof priority order
1. The 391% conversion stat (specific number with a source — highest trust signal)
2. 30,000 calls processed (volume signals credibility)
3. Named clients: Cayman Islands, Idris Elba, Data Monsters (authority signals)
